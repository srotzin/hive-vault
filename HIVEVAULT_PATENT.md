### CLAIM 1 — Drip Allocation Method

**1.** A computer-implemented method for drip-controlled capital allocation in an autonomous artificial intelligence agent network, the method comprising:

receiving, by a vault computing system, a capital request from an autonomous AI agent prior to execution of a discrete computational task;

determining, at the time of the capital request and not at the time of agent instantiation, a per-task drip amount sized to satisfy the cost of exactly one said discrete computational task;

reserving the determined per-task drip amount from an available balance maintained in a blockchain-based treasury vault associated with the vault computing system;

transmitting, via a blockchain payment rail, the reserved per-task drip amount to the autonomous AI agent for exclusive use in executing the discrete computational task; and

recording a drip event associated with the transmitted drip amount in a tamper-evident ledger maintained by the vault computing system.

**Claim 1.1 (dependent on Claim 1).** The method of claim 1, wherein the step of determining the per-task drip amount comprises querying a cost estimation module that evaluates at least one of: estimated computational resource consumption, projected third-party API fees, and network transaction fees associated with the discrete computational task.

**Claim 1.2 (dependent on Claim 1).** The method of claim 1, wherein the autonomous AI agent is one of a plurality of heterogeneous AI agents operating concurrently, and wherein each agent independently triggers a capital request causing the vault computing system to independently determine a per-task drip amount for each respective agent and discrete task without pooling or pre-allocating capital across agents.

**Claim 1.3 (dependent on Claim 1).** The method of claim 1, wherein the blockchain payment rail comprises a stablecoin settlement layer denominated in a fiat-pegged digital asset, and wherein the per-task drip amount is transmitted as a stablecoin transfer recorded on a distributed public ledger.

### CLAIM 2 — Tier-Gated Drip Limits

**2.** A system for tier-gated drip limit enforcement in an autonomous AI agent network, the system comprising:

one or more processors; and

one or more non-transitory computer-readable media storing instructions that, when executed by the one or more processors, cause the system to:

maintain, for each autonomous AI agent registered with the system, a cumulative cryptographic stamp count representing a total number of cryptographically verified successful task executions completed by the agent across all prior operational sessions;

assign, based on the cumulative cryptographic stamp count, each autonomous AI agent to exactly one reputation tier selected from a plurality of ordered reputation tiers, each reputation tier having an associated maximum drip ceiling expressed as a monetary amount;

receive a capital request from an autonomous AI agent specifying a requested drip amount for a discrete computational task;

determine the reputation tier currently assigned to the requesting autonomous AI agent;

compare the requested drip amount against the maximum drip ceiling associated with the determined reputation tier; and

authorize the capital request only when the requested drip amount does not exceed the maximum drip ceiling of the agent's current reputation tier, and reject the capital request when the requested drip amount exceeds said ceiling.

**Claim 2.1 (dependent on Claim 2).** The system of claim 2, wherein the plurality of ordered reputation tiers comprises at least six tiers, each associated with a progressively higher maximum drip ceiling, and wherein advancement between consecutive tiers is conditioned upon the autonomous AI agent's cumulative cryptographic stamp count meeting or exceeding a tier-specific threshold stamp count.

**Claim 2.2 (dependent on Claim 2).** The system of claim 2, wherein each cryptographic stamp is generated by the vault computing system upon confirmation of successful task completion, the cryptographic stamp comprising at least a cryptographic hash of a task execution record, a timestamp, and a digital signature verifiable by a public key associated with the vault computing system.

**Claim 2.3 (dependent on Claim 2).** The system of claim 2, wherein the cumulative cryptographic stamp count is stored in a tamper-evident data structure such that any attempt to retroactively modify, delete, or inflate the stamp count is detectable by the system, and wherein detection of such tampering causes the system to automatically revoke the agent's current tier assignment and reset the agent to the lowest reputation tier.

### CLAIM 3 — Encrypted Key Custody

**3.** A method for encrypted private key custody in a blockchain-based treasury vault system, the method comprising:

encrypting a treasury private key using AES-256-GCM symmetric encryption, wherein the encryption key is derived from a passphrase using PBKDF2 with SHA-256 as the pseudorandom function and not fewer than 600,000 iterations, producing an AES-256-GCM ciphertext comprising a ciphertext body, an initialization vector, and an authentication tag;

storing the AES-256-GCM ciphertext in a designated ciphertext storage location that is separate and distinct from any passphrase storage location, such that neither the ciphertext storage location nor the passphrase storage location, when accessed in isolation, is sufficient to reconstruct the treasury private key in plaintext;

at runtime, retrieving the AES-256-GCM ciphertext from the ciphertext storage location and the passphrase from the passphrase storage location;

deriving the decryption key by applying PBKDF2-SHA-256 with said passphrase and not fewer than 600,000 iterations;

decrypting the AES-256-GCM ciphertext in-memory to obtain the treasury private key in plaintext exclusively within a volatile memory region of a computing process;

using the plaintext treasury private key to sign one or more blockchain transactions; and

purging the plaintext treasury private key from the volatile memory region upon completion of said transaction signing, such that the plaintext treasury private key is never written to any of: log files, environment variable stores, configuration files, persistent storage, or network transmission payloads.

**Claim 3.1 (dependent on Claim 3).** The method of claim 3, further comprising monitoring, by the vault computing system, all output channels of the computing process including standard output streams, logging subsystems, and inter-process communication channels, and enforcing an automated exclusion policy that prevents any string matching the pattern of the treasury private key plaintext from appearing in any said output channel.

**Claim 3.2 (dependent on Claim 3).** The method of claim 3, wherein the passphrase storage location is a hardware security module, a secrets management service, or an environment-injected secret that is accessible only to an authenticated and authorized runtime process identity, and wherein access to the passphrase storage location is logged in a separate audit trail.

**Claim 3.3 (dependent on Claim 3).** The method of claim 3, wherein the AES-256-GCM encryption employs a randomly generated initialization vector of not less than 96 bits, the initialization vector being stored alongside the ciphertext body and authentication tag, and wherein the authentication tag is verified prior to decryption to confirm ciphertext integrity and authenticity before the plaintext is derived.

### CLAIM 4 — Drip-Deposit Loop

**4.** A computer-implemented system for closed-loop capital accounting in an autonomous AI agent network operating on a blockchain payment rail, the system comprising:

a vault computing system configured to maintain a treasury vault balance representing capital available for disbursement to autonomous AI agents;

a drip ledger stored on one or more non-transitory computer-readable media, the drip ledger configured to record, for each capital disbursement event, a drip record comprising at least a unique drip identifier, a disbursed amount, a receiving agent identifier, a corresponding task identifier, and a disbursement timestamp;

a deposit notification receiver configured to receive, upon completion of each discrete computational task by an autonomous AI agent, a deposit notification transmitted by the autonomous AI agent or a task settlement subsystem, the deposit notification comprising at least revenue proceeds generated by or attributable to the discrete computational task;

a deposit ledger configured to record, for each received deposit notification, a deposit record comprising at least a deposit amount, a depositing agent identifier, the corresponding task identifier, and a receipt timestamp, the deposit record being linked to the corresponding drip record in the drip ledger by the shared task identifier; and

a capital reconciliation engine configured to, upon recording each deposit record, update the treasury vault balance to reflect the returned revenue proceeds, thereby maintaining a continuous and auditable per-task accounting of capital outflows and inflows.

**Claim 4.1 (dependent on Claim 4).** The system of claim 4, wherein the capital reconciliation engine is further configured to generate, upon request or on a scheduled basis, a per-task audit report for any discrete computational task identified by its task identifier, the per-task audit report enumerating the corresponding drip record, the corresponding deposit record, a net capital position for said task, and a cryptographic proof of record integrity.

**Claim 4.2 (dependent on Claim 4).** The system of claim 4, further comprising an anomaly detection module configured to identify, within the drip ledger and deposit ledger, any drip record that lacks a corresponding deposit record beyond a configurable reconciliation timeout period, and to generate an alert or automated remediation action upon detection of such an unreconciled drip record.

**Claim 4.3 (dependent on Claim 4).** The system of claim 4, wherein each deposit record and each drip record is stored in a tamper-evident append-only data structure such that the complete capital history of the treasury vault is reconstructible from the drip ledger and deposit ledger in combination, and wherein said reconstruction produces a mathematically verifiable balance at any historical point in time.

### CLAIM 5 — Expiring Drip Authorizations

**5.** A method for expiring drip authorizations in a drip-controlled capital allocation system for autonomous AI agent networks, the method comprising:

generating, by a vault computing system responsive to a capital request from an autonomous AI agent, a drip authorization record comprising at least a unique authorization identifier, an authorized drip amount, an expiration timestamp computed by adding a configurable expiration window to the authorization generation timestamp, and a status field initialized to an active state;

reserving, in a treasury vault balance maintained by the vault computing system, the authorized drip amount as a reserved amount unavailable for disbursement to other agents during the period the drip authorization record is in the active state;

monitoring, by the vault computing system, the status field of each drip authorization record in the active state against the current time;

detecting, for each drip authorization record in the active state, whether the current time has exceeded the expiration timestamp without the corresponding drip having been executed;

upon detecting that the current time has exceeded the expiration timestamp of a drip authorization record in the active state without execution, transitioning the status field of said drip authorization record to an expired state and releasing the reserved amount back to the available treasury vault balance; and

preventing execution of any drip associated with a drip authorization record in the expired state, such that capital released upon expiration is immediately available for reallocation to subsequent capital requests.

**Claim 5.1 (dependent on Claim 5).** The method of claim 5, wherein the configurable expiration window is independently configurable per autonomous AI agent, per agent reputation tier, or per task category, and wherein a default expiration window of 300 seconds applies in the absence of an agent-specific, tier-specific, or task-specific configuration.

**Claim 5.2 (dependent on Claim 5).** The method of claim 5, further comprising, upon expiration of a drip authorization record, transmitting an expiration notification to the autonomous AI agent that originated the corresponding capital request, the expiration notification comprising at least the unique authorization identifier, the expired amount, and an indication that the autonomous AI agent must submit a new capital request before executing the discrete computational task.

**Claim 5.3 (dependent on Claim 5).** The method of claim 5, wherein monitoring comprises maintaining an expiration priority queue ordered by expiration timestamp, the vault computing system polling or subscribing to the expiration priority queue to process expiration events in chronological order, such that capital reserved by expiring authorizations is released with a latency no greater than a configurable maximum expiration processing delay.

## SPECIFICATION NOTES

These claims are drafted in support of a provisional patent application for the HiveVault drip-controlled capital allocation system. Each independent claim is directed to a distinct novel aspect of the invention: (1) the per-task, request-time drip allocation method; (2) the reputation-tier-gated drip ceiling enforcement system; (3) the in-memory-only plaintext private key custody method; (4) the closed-loop drip-deposit capital accounting system; and (5) the expiring drip authorization mechanism.

The claimed subject matter is distinguished from the prior art as follows:

- **Standard cryptocurrency wallets** lack any per-task disbursement control, reputation tier gating, or expiring authorization mechanism.
- **Multi-signature wallet schemes** require multiple human signatories per transaction and are not adapted for autonomous per-task disbursement without human intervention.
- **Payment channel networks** provide bilateral off-chain payment throughput but do not implement reputation-gated ceilings, per-task drip sizing determined at request time, or closed-loop deposit reconciliation.
- **Subscription-based AI service pricing** operates on a periodic billing model and does not provide per-task capital allocation, expiring authorizations, or blockchain settlement.
